Indira Gandhi Canal


This canal is near Giddarbaha, Panjub .

It starts from the Harike Barrage at Harike, a few kilometers below the confluence of the Satluj and Beas rivers in the Indian state of Punjab.

This project is one of the biggest irrigation project in the world.

It has began in 1958 as Rajasthan Canal.

The project which use water from the Pong dam consists of 215 km long Rajasthan feeder canal & 445 km long Rajasthan main canal lying entirely in Rajasthan.

It is the largest canal of India.

Uses of Hirakud Dam
  • It has provided irrigation facility to northwest region of Rajasthan a part of Thar desert.
  • It provides life to the deserts of rajasthan mainly the thar desert.
  • The project has ultimately irrigated about 14.5 lakh hectares.
